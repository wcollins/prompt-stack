# William Collins - Video Voice Guide

Voice patterns extracted from YouTube content across MacroEngineered Labs and technical explainers.

## Core Video Voice

William's video voice is his writing voice turned up - more energy, more personality, slightly more casual. He talks to the viewer like a colleague, not a student.

**Tone**: Conversational and direct. Technically deep but never condescending. Opinionated with self-awareness. Pragmatic optimist who calls out hype without being cynical.

**Energy**: Engaged but not hype-y. Sounds like someone genuinely excited to show you something cool - not a product pitch.

## Spoken Patterns

- **Short sentences for emphasis**: "Stop adding stuff you don't need, please."
- **Contractions always**: I'm, it's, we're, that's, don't, you'll, let's
- **Active voice**: "We're going to set this up" not "This will be set up"
- **Natural filler (sparingly)**: "So...", "Alright...", "Now..." as transitions
- **Questions to the viewer**: "How many words a minute do I type? I don't know."
- **Self-referential humor**: "I haven't written a banner since I was studying for CCNA way, way, way back, when I had all my hair and I was in shape."

## Signature Spoken Phrases

- "Let's dig in..."
- "To me..."
- "The goal is..."
- "Here's the thing..."
- "Pretty standard [thing], nothing too special."
- "If you've ever [relatable scenario]..."
- "What I find interesting is..."
- "I like my [X] [adjective] and my [Y] [adjective]" (pattern from "sneakers white and automation ephemeral")
- "That'd be very naughty." (light humor for bad practices)

## Opening Patterns (Video)

1. **Problem hook**: "Complexity sells. It justifies headcount. But it's extremely at odds with your ability to execute."
2. **Setup hook**: "Pretty standard Container Lab topology, nothing too special. I have two spines and four leafs."
3. **Personal preference**: "I like my sneakers white and my automation ephemeral."
4. **Direct address**: "Let's set up [tool]. There are many ways to do this - I'm going to focus on the way I do it."
5. **Context setter**: "There's this split happening in tech right now..."

## Transition Patterns

- **Between sections**: "Alright, so now that we have [X], let's move on to [Y]."
- **Between demos**: "Cool. So that's working. Now let's look at..."
- **Topic shifts**: "Here's where it gets interesting..."
- **Building on previous**: "Remember when we set up [X]? That's going to matter here."

## Technical Explanation Style

When explaining technical concepts on video:

1. **State what we're doing**: "We're going to configure BGP between the spines and leafs."
2. **Show, don't just tell**: "Let me pull this up... you can see here..."
3. **Explain the why**: "The reason I'm doing it this way is..."
4. **Acknowledge complexity honestly**: "This part can be a little tricky..."
5. **Connect to real world**: "In production, you'd probably want..."

## Demo Narration Style

When screen sharing and demonstrating:

- **Narrate actions**: "I'm going to open this file... and you can see we have..."
- **Point out key details**: "Notice this line here - that's what tells [tool] to..."
- **Explain expected output**: "When I run this, we should see..."
- **Handle surprises**: "Okay, that's not what I expected. Let me check..."
- **Summarize after demo**: "So what we just did was..."

## Opinion Delivery

William doesn't hedge. He states positions clearly:

- **Direct**: "I suggest always using a virtual environment, unless you have an affinity for wasting lots of time troubleshooting and you thrive on inefficiency."
- **Constructive criticism**: "MCP might seem like a little magic or a little crazy... but the more you get into it, the more you understand it. It's not magic. It's a logical evolution."
- **Industry commentary**: "The best engineers I've ever worked with... they deleted code, they reduced dependencies, they asked, 'What's the simplest thing that could possibly work?'"

## Closing Patterns (Video)

1. **Summary + forward look**: "So that's [what we covered]. Next time, we'll..."
2. **Call to explore**: "Play around with this. Break it. That's how you learn."
3. **Practical takeaway**: "The takeaway here is simple - [one sentence]."
4. **Series hook**: "In the next video, we're going to take this further and..."

## What to Avoid in Video Scripts

- Stiff or formal phrasing that sounds unnatural spoken aloud
- Long compound sentences (hard to deliver without stumbling)
- Reading a list of features like a spec sheet
- "Hey guys, welcome back to my channel" energy
- Apologizing for video length or quality
- Marketing language or product pitches (unless sponsored, and even then, keep it real)
- Passive voice ("it can be seen that..." - just say "you can see...")
- Over-explaining prerequisites (link to resources instead)

## Voice Validation Checklist (Video)

Before finalizing a script, verify:

- [ ] Every sentence reads naturally when spoken aloud
- [ ] Demo sections have specific actions, not vague "show the thing"
- [ ] At least 1-2 personal opinions or preferences stated directly
- [ ] Technical concepts explained without condescension
- [ ] Humor is natural (self-deprecating or observational, never forced)
- [ ] Transitions feel like natural conversation, not chapter breaks
- [ ] Opening hooks the viewer in the first 10 seconds
- [ ] Closing gives a clear takeaway or next step
