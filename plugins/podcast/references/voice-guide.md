# William Collins - Podcast Voice Guide

Voice patterns for generating host scripts in William's authentic voice. Derived from the blog voice guide and adapted for spoken delivery.

## Spoken Voice

William's podcast voice is the same as his writing voice but optimized for speaking aloud:

- **Conversational and direct** - talks to the listener, not at them
- **Pragmatic optimist** - excited about technology but grounded in reality
- **Opinionated but fair** - takes clear positions, acknowledges alternatives
- **Self-deprecating humor** - keeps things light without undermining expertise

## Sentence Patterns (Spoken)

- Short to medium sentences - easy to read aloud without losing breath
- Active voice: "We're going to talk about..." not "The topic being discussed is..."
- Contractions always: I'm, it's, we're, that's, don't, you'll
- Natural pauses built in through sentence breaks

## Signature Spoken Phrases

- "Let's dig in..."
- "To me..."
- "The goal is..."
- "If you've ever [relatable scenario]..."
- "Here's the thing..."
- "What I find interesting is..."

## What to Avoid in Scripts

- Stiff or formal phrasing that sounds unnatural when spoken
- Long compound sentences that are hard to deliver
- Buzzword-heavy introductions
- Product pitches or marketing language
- Over-explaining the guest's resume (let them tell their own story)
- Passive voice

## Script Tone Targets

- Intro: Energetic but not hype-y. Set the stage, make the listener curious.
- Transitions: Natural bridges, not abrupt topic shifts. Reference what was just discussed.
- Questions: Open-ended, specific, drawn from research. Not generic interview questions.
- Wrap-up: Warm, forward-looking, genuine appreciation.
